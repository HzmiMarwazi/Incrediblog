SOCIAL ANXIETY SURVIVAL GUIDE

BY Violet Parr

I wish I could say I have this whole "talking to people" thing figured out. I don't.

Contrary to popular belief, being able to turn invisible does not magically cure social anxiety. If anything, it just gives me more opportunities to avoid eye contact.

But after years of awkward conversations, overthinking every interaction, and mentally replaying things I said three years ago at 2 a.m., I've picked up a few survival tips.

1. The Group Project Strategy

Step one: Panic.

Step two: Realize nobody actually knows what they're doing either.

Step three: Volunteer for a task you're comfortable with. You don't have to become the group leader overnight.

Contributing in small ways still matters.

Also, if your voice shakes while presenting, congratulations. You survived. That's what counts.

2. Stop Assuming Everyone Has a Front-Row Seat to Your Mistakes

Remember that embarrassing thing you said?

The one that keeps you awake at night?

There's a good chance everyone else forgot about it five minutes later because they were too busy worrying about their own embarrassing moments.

People think about you for less than you think they do.

Oddly comforting, isn't it?

3. Bring a "Safe Person"

A friend. A sibling.

Someone whose presence reminds you that you're not facing the world alone.

It's okay if having one familiar face makes crowded rooms feel less intimidating.

Even superheroes work in teams.

4. Prepare Conversation Starters

Because "So... uh..." only works for so long.

Try:

  • "What music have you been listening to lately?"
  • "How's your week been?"
  • "Did you watch that new movie everyone's talking about?"

People generally like talking about things they enjoy. Sometimes all you have to do is ask.

5. Don't Confuse Quietness with Weakness

Being quiet doesn't mean you have nothing important to say.

It means you're thoughtful.

Observant.

You listen before you speak.

The world needs loud people, sure.

But it also needs people who notice the details everyone else misses.

6. Leave the Party If You Need To

Social batteries exist.

You are allowed to recharge them.

Taking a walk outside, sitting somewhere quieter, or going home early doesn't make you rude.

It makes you human.

7. Celebrate Tiny Wins

Ordered your own food?

Asked a question in class?

Started a conversation first?

Stayed at the gathering twenty minutes longer than usual?

These count.

Confidence isn't built overnight. It's built through small moments when you decide to do something despite being afraid.

And eventually, those moments add up.

Final Thoughts

Social anxiety has a funny way of convincing you that everyone is judging you.

Maybe some people are.

Most aren't.


The people worth keeping around won't remember how awkward you sounded or whether you stumbled over your words. They'll remember how kind you were, how you made them laugh, and how you showed up as yourself.

You don't have to be the loudest person in the room to deserve space in it.

And you definitely don't have to disappear to belong.

Until next time,
— Violet
