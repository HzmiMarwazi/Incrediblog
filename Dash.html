That’s when I noticed Jack-Jack staring at the window. At Mr. Johnson’s house next door. You know, the neighbor who always thinks our family is “a little strange.” Anyway.

So I decided to take him out just to look around the neighborhood.
That was the plan. Just look around. But then I saw Mr. Johnson’s garden. And I had an idea. A really good idea. So I brought Jack Jack a little closer. He was still staring at the garden like it was calling him or something. Then suddenly his eyes started doing that weird glow thing again. And before I even had time to think Boom. The garden went crazy. Not like exploding crazy. More like… everything started changing at once. Flowers bending in weird directions, garden gnomes looking confused, bushes moving like they were alive and trying to escape. It was actually kind of impressive. I didn’t even fix it right away. I just watched.

Because honestly… it was funny. Then Jack-Jack giggled like he was proud of it. That’s when I realized this was getting out of hand.
Too late. Mr. Johnson came outside. He stopped. Looked at his garden.

I fixed parts of the garden while moving, moved Jack-Jack out of sight, straightened things, and tried to make it all look like nothing weird ever happened. When I finally stopped, I was standing there like a normal kid again. Mr. Johnson just stared for a long time. Then he slowly went back inside without saying anything. Which is honestly worse than yelling. Because now I think he’s just confused forever. Anyway.

I think he liked it.
I think I did too.
That might be a problem.

Fast enough to be anywhere

Dear Blog Friends,

One of the best things about being super fast is that I never have to wait. Seriously. People spend hours planning trips, packing bags, and sitting on planes. Meanwhile, if I wanted to see a beach, I'd just run there. I'd probably start with Santa Monica Beach and race along the shoreline before anyone even noticed I was there. Plus, if someone dropped their ice cream, I could see it happen in slow motion. The kid would cry, the parents would panic, and I'd already be halfway down the beach before the first tear hit the sand. If I wanted to see a mountain, I'd run there too. Maybe I'd sprint up Yosemite's trails just to reach the top first. Everyone else would be taking photos of the view, but I'd probably be watching hikers argue about which trail they were supposed to take. One place I'd definitely visit is the Golden Gate Bridge. Not just to run across it.

I'd want to stop at the highest part and look down at everything happening below. From up there, you can see all kinds of funny stuff. Someone rushing to work, someone chasing a runaway hat, a tourist taking fifty pictures of the exact same thing, or a kid dropping an ice cream and immediately bursting into tears. People are hilarious when they don't know anyone is watching. I'd also love running through New York City.

There are always interesting things happening there. Street performers, people talking to themselves, taxis honking for no reason, and tourists staring ancients with maps while standing right in the middle of the sidewalk. I'd probably circle around them three times before they figured out where they were going. The Grand Canyon would be awesome too. Most people spend hours looking at it. I'd probably run from one viewpoint to another just to compare which side looked cooler. And of course, I'd visit Disney World. Not because of the rides. Because I'd finally get to see if the lines are actually as long as everyone says they are. Then I'd ride everything before most people moved forward one spot.

I think I'd spend most of my time exploring places I've never seen before. Not because I have to go there. Just because I can. Sometimes I wonder how far I could get before dinner. Probably pretty far. The hardest part would be remembering where I started.

— Dash ⚡
